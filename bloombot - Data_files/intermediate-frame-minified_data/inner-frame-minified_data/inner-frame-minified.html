Spurious Correlations

Correlation Does Not Imply Causation

Correlation Coefficient: 0.99
p-value < 0.01 (statistically significant)

⚠️ Important Note

These correlations are spurious! Just because two variables move together does not mean one causes the other. Common reasons for spurious correlations include:

  • Confounding variables (e.g., population growth, time trends)
  • Random coincidence with sufficient data points
  • Data dredging (testing many correlations until finding one)
  • Cherry-picking time periods or outliers
